Biography

Since 2016, Enrique Segura is Principal Conductor of the “Opal Sinfonietta” of Boulogne Sur Mer. With around 10 presentations every season, the orchestra presents to the public symphonic concerts, choral symphonic works, musicals and operettas.

In 2018 he creates and conducts, the “Orchestre Franco-Américain”, a project that engaged professionals and advanced students of music born in the American continent or in France, to produce several concerts all around the country.Thanks of his excellent and appreciated work last summer, he became the new orchestral conductor of the Lyric Festival in Samoëns, France. In the 2021 edition, he will conduct the Mozart’s opera “Die Zauberflöte”.

Segura conducts also several choirs in Paris : « Ensemble de Port Royal », « Chorale du Lundi », « Chorale Maîtres chanteurs », and an amateur orchestra : « Orchestre Symphonique du Chantier »

Passionate about versatility, his musical interests and repertoire range from symphonic, opera and choral music to Latin-American music. His interpretations on chorale symphonic works are well appreciated: Mozart’s Requiem, Faure’s Requiem, Saint-Saëns’s Requiem, and the Vivaldi’s Gloria, and also works such as Show Boat (J. Kern), La Belle Hélène (J. Offenbach), Mesdames de La Halle (J. Offenbach) among others.

He has a Bachelor of Music Degree specialized in Orchestral Conducting, at the Faculty of Fine Arts (University of La Plata, Argentina); under the guidance of Maestro Jorge de Larrañaga, Maestro Bernardo Teruggi and Maestra Eduviges Picone. He continued his studies at the Regional Music Conservatory in Reims, France, under the guidance of Maestra Rut Schereiner, and with Maestro Adrian McDonnell in the Schola Cantorum of Paris, where he finished his diploma with honors.
